Lost password

How you recover a password or user ID depends on how you are registered to sign in:

Sign-in with a CRA user ID

To verify your identify, you will need:

  • Your User ID
  • Answers to the security questions you made when you registered

Steps to create a new password

Follow these steps to create a new password for your account:

  1. Go to My Account for Individuals
  2. Select "Sign in with your CRA user ID and password"
  3. On the CRA sign-in page, select "Forgot your password?"
  4. Follow the instructions to create a new password
Sign-in through your financial institution or province

CRA does not have your password

When you use your online banking or provincial credential, the information you enter and the identity of the partner institution are not shared with the government service you are trying to access. Similarly, information about the government service being accessed is not shared with your sign-in partner. No personally identifying information or banking or provincial details are ever shared.

Change your password with your financial institution or province

As you are signing in through the financial institution or province, you will need to reset your password with that institution or province that you deal with.

Lost User ID

How you recover a password or user ID depends on how you are registered to sign in:

Sign-in with a CRA user ID

To verify your identify, you will need:

  • Social Insurance Number
  • Date of birth
  • Amount you entered on line 15000 of your income tax and benefit return, from one of the previous two tax years

Steps to create a new password

Follow these steps to create a new password for your account:

  1. Go to My Account for Individuals
  2. Select "Sign in with your CRA user ID and password"
  3. On the CRA sign-in page, select "Forgot your user ID?"
  4. Follow the instructions to recover your User ID
Cannot recover a User ID for Represent a Client or Tax information web service

If you have forgotten your CRA user ID for either Represent a Client or the Tax information web service, you cannot recover it.

If you lose your Uesr ID, you have to register again for a new sign-in credential.

Sign-in through your financial institution or province

CRA does not have your user ID

When you use your online banking or provincial credential, the information you enter and the identity of the partner institution are not shared with the government service you are trying to access. Similarly, information about the government service being accessed is not shared with your sign-in partner. No personally identifying information or banking or provincial details are ever shared.

Change your password with your financial institution or province

As you are signing in through the financial institution or province, you will need to recover your user ID with that institution or province.

Recover a revoked user ID or password

You may have received a notification informing you that a user ID and password you use to access CRA sign-in services have been revoked.

To regain access to your CRA account with a revoked user ID or password, try these options:

Option 1

Use a different sign in method:

If you have one, you may be able to sign in by using a different CRA user ID and password, your banking sign in, or using a provincial partner to access your CRA account.

Option 2

Register for a new CRA user ID:

When registering for a new CRA user ID and password, ensure that your user ID is unique to your CRA account. To help avoid having your CRA user ID and password revoked in the future, your new user ID and password should:

  1. not be similar to one that was previously revoked
  2. not contain any parts of your name.

Once registered, the CRA will mail you a security code to give you full access to online services. While waiting for your security code, you can still file your income tax return online using NETFILE certified software and apply for COVID-19 benefits.

How to regain access to your Careers candidate profile

If your CRA user ID and password for the Candidate Profile service have been revoked, you may complete a candidate profile recovery. This will allow the new CRA user ID and password you create during the recovery process to be linked to your existing candidate profile.

For details: Registration process to access the CRA sign-in services

Option 3

If neither option is available to you

You may not be able to use these options if your account is locked or your address on file is incorrect. If so, please call the CRA .

Locked out by security questions

If you have been locked out after incorrectly answering security questions, or if you do not remember the answers to your security questions, you will have to call to authenticate and have us unlock your account for you.

Call the CRA to unlock your account.
