Sign-in Partner

Follow these steps to register for My Account with Interac Sign-in Partner.

  1. Ensure your bank or credit union is participating

    You must have an account with one of our Sign-in Partner banks or credit unions.

  2. Gather your documents

    Make sure you have the following ready:

    • Your online banking sign in credentials
    • Your Social Insurance Number
      • Alternatives: a Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN)
    • Your date of birth
    • Your current postal code or ZIP code
    • Your assessed tax returns for the 2 most recent tax years
      • If you don't have either: You may still be able to sign in with a provincial partner.

    After you sign in at your bank, you will come back to the CRA site. You will have 15 minutes to confirm your identity based on these documents.

  3. Register with a Sign-in Partner

    Once you have gathered all your documents:

    Register with a Sign-in partner

    Ensure your account privacy on a shared device

    Don't use auto-complete with your bank sign in on a shared device

    If you register for My Account on a device you share with other people, make sure you don't use your browser's auto-complete for your online banking sign in. If you sign in with someone else's banking credentials and link your SIN number to that, the other person will have access to your account.

    Your information is not shared

    Your banking ID and password are never shared with the CRA

    When you use your online banking credential, the information you enter and the identity of the financial institution are not shared with the government service you are trying to access.

    The Sign-In Partner does not provide any identity information to the Government and simply validates your username or card number and password.

CRA User ID

Follow these steps to register for My Account with a CRA User ID and password.

  1. Gather your documents

    When you register, there is a 15 minute time limit to prevent security risks of unattended devices. Make sure you have the following ready:

    • Your Social Insurance Number
      • Alternatives: a Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN)
    • Your date of birth
    • Your current postal code or ZIP code
    • Your assessed tax returns for the 2 most recent tax years
      • If you don't have either: Your tax return for at least one of the two most recent tax years must have been filed and assessed for you to register.

  2. Register with a new CRA User ID and password

    Once you have gathered all your documents:

    Create a CRA user ID
    How to choose memorable security questions

    You will be asked to determine 5 security questions and answers that will be used to recover your account if you forget your password.

    You may want to choose questions that have more factual answers, instead of questions that can change over time or can have more than one answer.

    For example, it may be easier to remember the make of your first car, instead of your favourite food or the name of a childhood friend that you may sometimes write as a nickname or with their last name.

  3. Wait for your CRA security code

    Once you complete the first step of your registration, a CRA security code will be sent to you by mail. This will take 7 to 14 days.

    Before the expiry date, sign in with your Sign-in Partner and follow the online instructions to enter your security code.

    Keep your security code on file

    The CRA security code you receive expires after 16 months. If you're ever locked out, you can use your security code to recover your account.

    Get a new CRA security code

    If you lose your CRA security code or it expires and you have not completed your registration, you will have to get a new one.

    Get limited access without a security code

    You can get limited access immediately, by beginning the registration process. You do not need to enter the CRA security code to access the following services:

    • View status of your tax return
    • Notification preferences
    • Request a remittance voucher
    • View notice of assessment or reassessment
    • View RRSP deduction limit and TFSA contribution room
    • Pay by pre-authorized debit (create new agreement)
Provincial partner

Follow these steps to sign in to My Acocunt with Alberta.ca Account or a BC Services Card.

  1. Ensure your province is participating

    You can sign in with either an Alberta.ca Account or BC Service Card.

  2. Register with your province

    If you're not yet registered with your province, you will need to register with them first.

  3. File a tax return

    You must have a tax return assessed before you can access My Account. This can be for any previous tax year.

  4. Sign in right away

    You don't need to register with the CRA. You can sign in to My Account as long as you have a provincial partner sign in.

    Sign in with a provincial partner

  5. Register with another method as well

    Signing in with a provincial partner can give you immediate access to most of My Account without needing to wait for a security code.

    However, you won't be able to use certain advanced functions of My Account, such as:

    • Autofill my Return: some tax softwares will ask you to sign in to My Account to auto-complete your tax return
    • Express Notice of Assessment: some tax softwares will ask you to sign in to My Account to give you a Notice of Assessment sooner
    • Sign in to My Business Account or Represent a Client: you cannot access a business or representative account with a provincial partner
